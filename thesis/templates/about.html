About us

The study "Predictive Analytics of Colleges’ Admission Slot Availability Using Classification Algorithm" aimed to help Palawan State University manage program slot allocations by predicting slot availability. Researchers compared four algorithms—Random Forest, Logistic Regression, Support Vector Machine (SVM), and Gaussian Naïve Bayes—using a confusion matrix to assess their effectiveness. The Random Forest algorithm emerged as the most accurate, achieving 97.38% accuracy and demonstrating high precision with a Mean Absolute Error of 53.14 and a Root Mean Squared Error of 88.94. The findings suggest that predictive analytics, especially using Random Forest, can enhance decision-making and resource allocation in the university's admissions process.
